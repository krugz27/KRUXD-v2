**User Problem:** Analysts spent hours reconciling fragmented fuel data, couldn’t make timely trading or operational decisions.

**Business Goal:** Launch a differentiated intelligence platform that helps fuel suppliers optimize supply, pricing, and customer performance, driving customer retention and opening new revenue opportunities in the energy sector

## Context

Fuel suppliers often manage pricing, inventory, and customer performance across fragmented systems. Teams were forced to move between spreadsheets, BI exports, and internal tools to answer basic operational questions.

The opportunity was to create a single intelligence hub that supported daily decisions while also giving leadership a clearer picture of retention risk and revenue opportunities.

## Process Snapshot (Text-led Block)

### Approach

1. Mapped recurring operational decisions and the data needed to support each one.
2. Audited existing analytics surfaces and identified hierarchy / navigation failures.
3. Prototyped alternative dashboard structures and tested comprehension with users.
4. Sequenced delivery into phases so high-value workflows shipped first.

### Collaboration

- Product: prioritization, roadmap sequencing, success metrics
- Engineering: data availability, performance constraints, implementation strategy
- Operations / Account teams: validation of workflows, terminology, and decision timing

## Outcomes (Metrics + Supporting Visual Placeholder)

- Reduced time-to-insight for recurring operational reviews
- Improved clarity of customer performance and retention risk signals
- Fewer support questions tied to dashboard interpretation
- Stronger alignment between account, pricing, and supply stakeholders
