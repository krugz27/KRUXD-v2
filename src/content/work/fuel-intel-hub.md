## Problem + Goal

**User Problem:** Analysts spent hours reconciling fragmented fuel data and could not make timely trading or operational decisions.

**Business Goal:** Launch a differentiated intelligence platform that helps fuel suppliers optimize supply, pricing, and customer performance, driving customer retention and opening new revenue opportunities in the energy sector.

## Exploration: Version 1

Our first wireframe direction emphasized operational oversight and regional monitoring.

**What we tested:** Could analysts anchor on a broad overview dashboard to triage supply risk and competitor pressure across multiple terminals?

**What we learned:** While this view was valuable for identifying risks, it largely positioned the tool as a monitoring dashboard. Users wanted to go beyond "what is happening" to explore "what should I do about it?"

<div class="cs-split">
  <div>
    <p>
      This exploration helped validate the usefulness of a regional overview, but it also revealed a
      positioning problem: users saw insight, but not enough support for action.
    </p>
    <p>
      The takeaway was not to discard operational visibility, but to make it a foundation for better
      decision support rather than the primary value proposition.

## Exploration: Version 2

Our second iteration shifted from reporting toward decision support by connecting operational data directly to pricing and margin levers.

**What we tested:** Could supply and pricing managers use this hub to simulate scenarios, optimize prices, and align supply actions with profitability goals?

**What we learned:** Analysts saw this as moving from a passive tool to an intelligence platform, helping them make proactive, financially grounded decisions rather than just track stock levels.

<div class="cs-split">
  <div>
    <p>
      This direction reframed the product from a dashboard into a working tool for operational and
      commercial decisions. Users responded to the tighter relationship between signals, scenarios, and
      recommended actions.
    </p>
    <p>
      The shift also clarified the product story internally: this was not just better reporting, it was
      a platform for decision-making.
    </p>

## Initial prototypes

### Prototype: Version 1

While early wireframes explored whether analysts could triage supply risks and competitor pressure using broad overviews, the refined concepts, as seen in these first prototypes, doubled down on actionable intelligence, moving from static monitoring dashboards to scenario-driven decision support tools where supply managers and pricing managers could not only see risks but also test and implement solutions.

_Note: These early prototypes used v1 of our Eco design system._

<div class="cs-split">
  <div>
    <p>

      balance between operational context and pricing actions before full visual system refinement.

This approach let participants experience core workflows, give immediate feedback, and enabled us to rapidly iterate live in the sessions, validating concepts and refining priorities before moving into full-scale design and development.
